I have adapted the implementation so that the PID gains for the steer and throttle controller are read from a json file, namely pid_gains.json, so that I could evaluate different gains without recompiling the project. 
I tried the following experiments - they follow the following convention
E\d: Steer[kp, ki, kd], Throttle[kp, ki, kd]

E1: Steer[0.1, 0.0, 0.0], Throttle[0.1, 0.0, 0.0]
Plots: E1_steer.png, E1_throttle.png
Observation: In the simulator, the ego vehicle drove straight into the vehicle ahead in its lane. There was light steering right before collision. This indicates that kp for Steering is too low. This is also reflected in the 'Steering Output' curve which stays around zero, and the error which fluctuates very strongly in both directions. So the kp for steering needs to be increased.  
The throttle output is proportional to the error, as expected for P-only control. Since the vehicle still collides, the throttle controller may be too aggressive relative to the weak steering response, so I keep throttle modest while tuning steering.


E2: Steer[0.5, 0.0, 0.0], Throttle[0.3, 0.0, 0.0]
Plots: E2_steer.png, E2_throttle.png
Observation: In the simulator, the vehicle succesfully changed lanes once and avoided hitting a vehicle straight ahead in its lane. However, the second time around, it did not steer strongly enough. The throttle behavior was stronger this time, but the driving was a bit jerky.
In E2_steer.png, I observe that the error magnitude stayed comparable to E1, but the curve became 'sharper'. This indicates a rapidly changing error. Adding a kd term to steering control now will probably help.
The throttle behavior was also slightly better in the sim, but I will hold E2 params in E3 to refine only steering.

E3: Steer[0.5, 0.0, 0.2], Throttle[0.3, 0.0, 0.0]
